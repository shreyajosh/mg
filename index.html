Why is an Inventory Management System Vital for SMBs?

Keeping track of inventory is essential to avoiding surplus, satisfying customer demands, and anticipating when to restock a particular product. It directly impacts the bottom line, and a scalable firm cannot function up to the mark without a sound inventory management system.

Inventory mismanagement can prove to be catastrophic. For example, in the last year itself, a whopping 80 percent+ of customers worldwide suffered out-of-stock situations, resulting in a business loss of more than $1.75 trillion. The core reason - it’s unmanaged/poorly managed inventory.

Optimization of the Supply Chain for Maintenance, Repairs, and Operations (MRO)

A facility, whether a factory, office or warehouse, is essential to every company's operation. Like a broken machine, construction flaws, errors, and malfunctions can halt output. Maintenance, repair, and operations (MRO) encompass a wide range of areas, including but not limited to electricals or cleaning. It refers to all procedures and actions necessary for the maintenance of a facility and its systems.

Facility managers use MRO supplies to ensure that the property in their hand operates with its best possible shape intact.

Preventive Maintenance’s Significance in Supply Chain Management

Generally, facilities go for fixing something only after it’s broken. This deferred maintenance approach exposes facilities to increased expenses and reduced productivity. In fact, any manufacturing or industrial facility cannot function reliably and sustainably without regular maintenance. Even while modern tools and machines have come a long way, they are still susceptible to the same wear and tear as those of the past. However, modern advancements in production and maintenance techniques have made substantial differences in the situation now, and preventive maintenance is one of those strategies.

The increased longevity of assets is undoubtedly the most significant advantage of preventative maintenance.
